<br>When Singapore became independent in 1965, close to 1 million people staked
their future on the nation and registered as Singapore’s first citizens.
Today, <strong><a href="https://www.facebook.com/FoundersMemorialSingapore?__cft__[0]=AZVD5g5BF4I3XZohaqqtCyILVTMRoSLagwU_FA6uPYbGVkUEki8-SQr6PvBcE1KNLsHvzXobhLEQ1Sn3MZi4_6-wd_42RsAqzPnjrE0jkuLx1dQFi8QBCyc_BUYbGN6X92sOPsOxzmhCNu4E5kUuIT25_JmytONcZjsGQDqu5NZThf958H8npRirt0v3z-FDc8Q&amp;__tn__=-]K-R" class="x1i10hfl xjbqb8w x1ejq31n x18oe1m7 x1sy0etr xstzfhl x972fbf x10w94by x1qhh985 x14e42zd x9f619 x1ypdohk xt0psk2 x3ct3a4 xdj266r x14z9mp xat24cr x1lziwak xexx8yu xyri2b x18d9i69 x1c1uobl x16tdsg8 x1hl2dhg xggy1nq x1a2a7pz xkrqix3 x1sur9pj x1fey0fg x1s688f" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">Founders' Memorial Singapore</a></strong> pays
tribute to our founding generation through Project Citizen - The First
Million, a year-long initiative that comprises a senior engagement programme,
Project Citizen pop-ups and a creative showcase ‘Not Mere Spectators: The
Makings of Multicultural Singapore’ at <strong><a href="https://www.facebook.com/nationalgallerysingapore?__cft__[0]=AZVD5g5BF4I3XZohaqqtCyILVTMRoSLagwU_FA6uPYbGVkUEki8-SQr6PvBcE1KNLsHvzXobhLEQ1Sn3MZi4_6-wd_42RsAqzPnjrE0jkuLx1dQFi8QBCyc_BUYbGN6X92sOPsOxzmhCNu4E5kUuIT25_JmytONcZjsGQDqu5NZThf958H8npRirt0v3z-FDc8Q&amp;__tn__=-]K-R" class="x1i10hfl xjbqb8w x1ejq31n x18oe1m7 x1sy0etr xstzfhl x972fbf x10w94by x1qhh985 x14e42zd x9f619 x1ypdohk xt0psk2 x3ct3a4 xdj266r x14z9mp xat24cr x1lziwak xexx8yu xyri2b x18d9i69 x1c1uobl x16tdsg8 x1hl2dhg xggy1nq x1a2a7pz xkrqix3 x1sur9pj x1fey0fg x1s688f" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">National Gallery Singapore</a></strong>.</p>
<p>Together, these initiatives call on us to reflect: What it meant to be
a citizen when Singapore was just finding its footing. What thoughts, feelings
and dilemmas did they face, and how did this conscious choice shape our
identity and sense of belonging as a nation.</p>
<p>By capturing memories, sparking conversations, and bringing stories closer
to the community, Project Citizens reminds us that our nation was built
not only by leaders, but also by everyday people who embodied resilience,
boldness and multiculturalism.</p>
<p>Project Citizens will find a permanent home at the Founders’ Memorial,
when it opens in 2028.</p>
