<p>From its humble beginnings as a settlement for early Chinese immigrants
to the vibrant hub it is today, Chinatown remains a neighbourhood rich
in resilience, culture, and community.</p>

pushcarts offering local delicacies.</p>
<p>Chinatown is a melting pot of cultures. While predominantly shaped by
Chinese immigrants, the area was also home to other communities. Indian

and Upper Cross Street, dealing in textiles, spices, and daily essentials.
Mosques like Jamae Mosque, stand alongside Hindu temples like Sri Mariamman
Temple, reflecting the rich tapestry of cultures and faiths that have coexisted
in Chinatown for generations.</p>
